Pros and Cons of Free VPNs

Let’s break it down—the good, the bad, and the ugly when it comes to free VPNs.

Pros:

  • They’re free (obviously). Perfect for casual use like streaming geo-blocked shows or basic browsing.
  • Some free VPNs (like ProtonVPN and Windscribe) offer decent security without compromising your data.
  • No long-term financial commitment—just download and go.

Cons:

  • Limited server locations and slower speeds compared to paid VPNs.
  • Data limits (e.g., TunnelBear gives you just 2GB per month).
  • Potentially shady practices: some free VPNs sell your data or bombard you with ads.
  • Lack of advanced features like split tunneling or better encryption protocols.

Should You Use Free VPNs? It depends. If you’re just looking to change your location for streaming or basic browsing, free VPNs can be a lifesaver. But if your primary concern is security—like for sensitive transactions or protecting personal data—invest in a reputable paid VPN. The peace of mind is totally worth it.
